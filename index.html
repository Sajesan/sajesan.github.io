Sajesan Aryal

Graduate Student

Univeristy of Alabama at Birmingham

I am a second year doctoral student working with Dr. Rui Lu at the University of Alabama at Birmingham (UAB), under the program of Genetics, Genomics, and Bioinformatics (GGB). My primary research interest is understanding the epigenetic regulation by leveraging state-of-the-art functional genomics tools. Using a combination of CRISPR/Cas9 genome editing, deep sequencing of the epigeneic modifications, genome-wide epigenomic profiling, single-cell transcriptomics, and integrative bioinformatics analysis, my work centers on understanding the stepwise distinct epigenetic modifications that underlie the emergence of acute myeloid leukemia (AML), critical oncogenic pathways, and use the knowledge to develop targeted therapeutic approaches.

I am highly interested in studying how complex patterns of high-throughput (-omics) data can be integrated and analyzed together to understand the complex biology. I primirily use R programming for data cleaning, data wrangling, visualization and exploratory analysis. I also write linux scripts and Snakemake workflows for building in house NGS pipelines. I regularly update my analysis notes and snakemake pipelines for processing genomic data in my blog posts and in my github repository. [My Curriculum Vitae]
